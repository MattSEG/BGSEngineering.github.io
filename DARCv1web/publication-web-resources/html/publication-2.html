Assembly Velocity (Molded-In Hardware): The DARC process allows for the direct integration of threaded interfaces and structural metallic hardware during the primary molding cycle. This eliminates the “drill-and-bond” phase, reducing total airframe assembly man-hours.

Modular Logistics & Field Repair: Unlike permanently bonded composite structures, DARC components with integrated hardware enable bolted assembly architectures. This allows airframes to be shipped in “flat-pack” configurations for high-density transport and rapidly assembled or repaired in the field using standard hand tools.

Superior Specific Strength: While 6061-T6 Aluminum (2.70 g/cm³) imposes a rigid “weight floor,” DARC (1.37 g/cm³) provides nearly double the specific strength (strength-to-weight ratio). Our current conservative baseline is 280 MPa, with process optimization in the upcoming Q1 2026 series targeted to yield further increases.
